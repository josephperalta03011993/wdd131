Bulacan, Philippines

The Philippines, an archipelago in Southeast Asia with over 7,600 islands, is home to more than 110 million people. Its history includes early Negrito inhabitants, Austronesian migrations, and influences from Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam, and Chinese trade. Spanish colonization began in the 16th century, followed by periods of American rule and Japanese occupation during World War II. The country gained independence in 1946 and later endured martial law under Ferdinand Marcos. Today, the Philippines is a unitary presidential republic facing challenges like inequality and corruption. Its economy is evolving from agriculture to service and manufacturing, and it's known for its biodiversity and vulnerability to natural disasters.
